## Purpose limits

These limits restate product-boundary rules from `AGENTS.md` and `SPEC.md` section 9 as binding pilot commitments. They are not optional and are listed in the participant notice:

- no productivity, performance, attendance-compliance, or risk scoring;
- no ranking of employees and no measurement of human worth or closeness;
- no automated messaging to participants;
- no use of pilot data for management decisions about individuals;
- no disclosure of dietary or accessibility detail beyond the least-disclosure brief to people who need it for catering;
- no transfer of pilot data outside the controller's chosen storage;
- no reuse of pilot data for a later purpose without a new recorded decision and notice.

## Article 6 legal basis

The controller must choose and record the basis. Points the decision must address, given an employment context:

- **Consent (6(1)(a))** — the employer–employee power imbalance makes freely given consent hard to establish; if chosen, participation must be genuinely voluntary, refusal must have no consequences, withdrawal must be easy, and the notice must say so.
- **Legitimate interests (6(1)(f))** — requires a recorded balancing test weighing the controller's interest in safe, accurate catering against participants' interests and expectations; the least-disclosure design and purpose limits are relevant mitigations.
- Other bases (contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task) are unlikely to fit a voluntary pilot and, if claimed, need specific justification.

The Article 6 basis does not remove the need for a separate special-category condition; that is recorded in [special-category-condition.md](special-category-condition.md).
