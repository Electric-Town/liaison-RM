# Code of conduct

Liaison RM contributors are expected to make the project safe to use and safe to contribute to.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss the work, evidence, and trade-offs without attacking the person.
- Use precise language and ask for clarification rather than assigning motives.
- Respect privacy, consent, accessibility needs, cultural differences, names, pronouns, and communication preferences.
- Give review feedback that identifies the risk, affected user, and practical correction.
- Credit prior work and disclose material conflicts of interest.
- Keep real personal or workplace data out of public issues, pull requests, fixtures, screenshots, and demonstrations.
- Make room for contributors who communicate asynchronously, need more processing time, or prefer written structure.

## Unacceptable conduct

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualised attention, doxxing, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private data or security details without permission.
- Using access, status, technical vocabulary, or review volume to intimidate contributors.
- Deliberately obscuring generated work, fabricated testing, copied material, or undisclosed conflicts.
- Retaliation against someone who reports a safety, privacy, accessibility, security, or conduct concern.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to an Electric Town maintainer. A maintainer involved in the concern must recuse themselves. Responses may include clarification, a warning, content removal, temporary participation limits, or permanent removal from project spaces.

The project will publish governance actions when useful for accountability while withholding personal or security-sensitive details. Bad-faith or retaliatory reports are themselves violations.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository discussions, project meetings, community spaces, release activity, and private communication undertaken in a project role.
